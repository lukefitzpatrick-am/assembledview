# Context sources - client, market, macro

Search these before writing commentary. Cite the specific source and period when used. Never quote a macro figure from memory - always search for the current value.

## Client ring
- Assembled View: the media plan, pacing screens, KPI targets, billing/flighting for the period.
- Project knowledge / client folders: objectives, briefs, prior reports and commentary, meeting notes, promo calendars.
- The client's own signals: site promotions, price changes, PR, product launches during the period.

## Market ring (campaign-period updates)
- Competitive spend and SOV: Nielsen Ad Intel (most comprehensive AU cross-platform monitoring; free category reports), Guideline SMI (agency-invoice sourced, >95% of AU agency market).
- Share of search: Google Trends brand vs category terms as the cheap ESOV/brand-effect indicator.
- Platform changes: Google core algorithm updates (annotate dates before attributing organic/search shifts), Google Ads product changes (AI Max, ACA), Meta delivery changes (Andromeda, Advantage+ defaults), seasonal auction inflation (Q4 CPMs typically 30-60% above Q1; cheap "Q5" window after Christmas).
- Category seasonality and cultural moments (AU): EOFY, Black Friday/Cyber, Boxing Day, back to school, footy finals, Melbourne Cup, election cycles (inflate CPMs), weather events.

## Macro ring (Australia)
- Westpac-Melbourne Institute Consumer Sentiment (monthly, mid-month; 100 = optimists equal pessimists).
- NAB Monthly Business Survey (confidence and conditions).
- ABS: Retail Trade, Monthly Household Spending Indicator (goods vs services split is useful), CPI.
- RBA cash rate decisions and guidance.
- Category-relevant extras: building approvals (home/retail categories), tourism data (travel), new vehicle sales (auto).

## How to use context honestly
- Context explains, it does not excuse. Pair every external factor with what we did about it.
- Rule out internal causes before reaching for external ones, and vice versa - state which alternatives were considered.
- One or two context points per report. The client needs signal, not an economics lecture.
