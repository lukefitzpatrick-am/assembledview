# Operational decision rules

The brain applied to day-to-day choices. Cite these in planning rationale and commentary.

## Budget and portfolio

1. Start at 60:40 brand-to-activation. Adjust: bigger brand / subscription / low consideration = more brand (up to ~70%+); B2B = ~46:54; heavy promo category or immediate cash crisis = temporarily more activation with an explicit rebuild plan.
2. Set an ESOV target on any growth brief. Expect ~0.5 share points per year per 10 ESOV points as the base rate; strong creative multiplies it. State the SOV measurement source (Nielsen Ad Intel, Guideline SMI, share of search).
3. Track share of search monthly as the leading indicator of brand effects; branded search volume is the visible residue of upper-funnel work in a zero-click world.

## Audience and reach

4. Define the audience as all category buyers within the purchase cycle. Resist narrowing below that unless physical availability genuinely limits who can buy (geography, licensing, age).
5. Maximise unduplicated 1+ reach per purchase cycle; cap frequency (typically 1-2 per week) before buying depth. Target light and non-buyers by default.
6. Plan against Category Entry Points: list the buying situations, check the plan covers the media moments and messages attached to each.

## Flighting

7. Continuity over burst. Prefer always-on or drumbeat. If forced to flight, shorten gaps rather than dropping weekly weight below the effective floor. Never fully dark on a growth brand.

## Channel and inventory arbitration

8. Judge inventory on attention-adjusted cost: aCPM = CPM / attentive seconds per 1,000 impressions (or Adelaide AU-weighted CPM). Cheap-but-empty loses to fairly-priced-and-attentive.
9. Apply attention floors, not attention maximisation: clear ~1.5-2.5 seconds of active attention (the lower bound applies when distinctive assets land in the first moments). Past the floor, buy more reach.
10. Favour 100% screen coverage and sound-on where available. A fully watched 6-second ad beats a partially watched 20-second ad. Treat sub-threshold formats as passive-frequency builders, not brand builders.

## Creative and copy

11. Brand within the first 1.5-2 seconds. Distinctive assets early and often. The asset's job is to brand the ad.
12. Emotional creative for brand work; clear, direct, offer-led copy for activation. Never judge brand creative on click metrics.
13. Rotate executions, keep the platform. Wear-out is slower than clients fear; consistency compounds (System1/IPA Compound Creativity direction - exact uplifts below 90% confidence).

## Measurement and commentary framing

14. Activation effects: expect fast response, decay within ~6 months, visible in platform metrics. Brand effects: expect 6+ month lags, visible in branded search, share of search, baseline sales drift and pricing power.
15. Never attribute a brand-building buy's success or failure to short-window CPA. Say what effect the money bought and measure it on the right clock.
16. When results move, check the theory-consistent mechanical explanations first: reach delivered vs planned, frequency distribution, attention quality of the placements, creative branding, competitive ESOV shifts, then external demand factors.
