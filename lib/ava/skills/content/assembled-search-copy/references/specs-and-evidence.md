# RSA specs and evidence base (verified July 2026)

## Specs (Google Ads Help, RSA documentation)
- Headlines: up to 15, 30 chars (min 3). Descriptions: up to 4, 90 chars (min 2). Display paths: 2 x 15 chars. Double-width languages count each char as 2.
- Serving: up to 3 headlines + 2 descriptions render; fewer often show. H2, H3, D2 never guaranteed. Compliance text must pin to H1, H2 or D1.
- Enhanced flexibility (standard since 2024-25): Google may show headline text at the start of descriptions, use unused headlines as sitelink-style assets, and borrow assets from other RSAs in the ad group (sensitive verticals excluded). Every asset must stand alone.
- Google recommends 2 RSAs per ad group (max 3); claims +6.6% conversions moving 1 to 2.

## 2025-26 platform changes
- AI Max for Search (GA 2025): bundles search term matching, text customization (renamed automatically created assets - generates headlines/descriptions from landing page), final URL expansion. Google claims ~7% more conversions at similar CPA/ROAS.
- Forced migrations: from September 2026, campaigns using ACA or campaign-level broad match auto-upgrade to AI Max; DSA upgrades run to early 2027. Text customization can be toggled off in the Asset optimization panel.
- Text guidelines (rolled out Q1 2026): natural-language brand voice instructions and banned-term lists constraining Google's generated text - use for regulated clients. Pinning still works under AI Max.
- Call ads being replaced by RSAs with call assets. Real-Time Policy Reviews give instant policy feedback.

## Evidence (Optmyzr studies: 13k+ accounts, 2M+ RSAs; 1M+ ad creative study)
- Ad Strength does not correlate with CPA/CVR/ROAS. "Average" ads frequently beat "Excellent". Treat as serving-eligibility heuristic only. (Google's counter-claim of +15% clicks Poor-to-Excellent is best read as more combinations = more auctions entered.)
- Pinning: fully pinned ads show slightly higher CTR but worse CPA/CVR. Pin only for compliance; pin 2-3 assets per position when forced.
- Headlines under ~20 chars: roughly half the CPA and higher CTR in aggregate (directional, do not quote as universal benchmarks).
- Sentence case beat Title Case on every metric in the 1M-ad study (correlational; likely brand-mix confounds; below 90% confidence on causality; free to adopt).
- DKI/customizers: raise impressions, tend to lower conversions per ad. Use only for genuinely large inventories.
- Google creative guidance: write the first 3 headlines as if shown together; most headlines distinct value propositions, not keyword permutations; avoid near-duplicates.
