# The three texts: laws, evidence and current refinements

Full grounding for the ten commitments. Use when a client output needs the evidence stated precisely, or when challenged.

## 1. How Brands Grow - Sharp / Ehrenberg-Bass

Empirical generalisations from the NBD-Dirichlet model of buying:

- **Double Jeopardy.** Smaller brands have far fewer buyers who are also slightly less loyal. Loyalty is a function of share, not an independent lever.
- **Retention Double Jeopardy.** All brands lose buyers in proportion to how many they have. Small brands cannot out-retain big ones. Growth is penetration.
- **Duplication of Purchase.** A brand shares customers with every other brand in proportion to those brands' shares. No meaningful "brand tribes" in most categories.
- **Pareto is ~60/20, not 80/20.** The heaviest 20% of buyers deliver roughly 50-60% of volume. The light half still delivers a large minority of sales, and light/non-buyers are where growth comes from.
- **Law of Buyer Moderation.** Heavy buyers regress to lighter buying in later periods. Do not over-read heavy-buyer panels.
- **Natural Monopoly Law.** Bigger brands over-index among light category buyers.
- **User bases seldom differ.** Competing brands sell to near-identical profiles. Attitudes follow behaviour, not the reverse.
- **Mental availability** is propensity to be noticed or thought of in buying situations, built across **Category Entry Points** (CEPs - Romaniuk). **Physical availability** is being easy to find and buy. These are the two engines of growth.
- **Distinctive assets** are measured on Fame x Uniqueness (Distinctive Asset Grid). They brand the ad; they do not differentiate the product.
- Media implications: reach over frequency, continuity over bursts, slow wear-out, sophisticated mass marketing.
- **HBG Part 2** extends the laws to services, B2B, luxury and emerging markets. Romaniuk's Better Brand Health Tracking (2023) is the current measurement playbook: mental market share, mental penetration, network size across CEPs.

## 2. The Long and the Short of It - Binet & Field

From ~996 IPA Effectiveness Databank campaigns:

- **Two effects, two speeds.** Activation spikes fast, decays within ~6 months. Brand building is slower, works via broad-reach emotional priming, and compounds - the majority of profit is long-term.
- **60:40** brand-to-activation maximises effectiveness on average. Explicitly not an iron rule (Binet's own words). Context adjustments (Effectiveness in Context, 2018): bigger brands, online/subscription models and low-consideration categories skew to ~70%+ brand; B2B optimum ~46:54 (Binet & Field for the LinkedIn B2B Institute, 2019); heavily promoted price-driven categories skew activation.
- **ESOV.** Growth is proportional to share of voice minus share of market: roughly +0.5% market share per year per 10 points of ESOV (Nielsen replication on 123 brands found a similar coefficient). Creative quality multiplies the coefficient severalfold.
- **Emotion beats rationality long term.** Emotional campaigns are ~2x as profitable as rational ones over 3+ years. Fame (talkability) campaigns are the most profitable of all.
- **Penetration beats loyalty in the IPA data too** - penetration-led campaigns ~3x more likely to report very large business effects. Convergent with Sharp.
- **Share of Search** (Binet, EffWorks 2020): share of category Google search volume predicts market share with a ~6-12 month lead depending on category (below 90% confidence on exact lead times per category).

## 3. The Attention Economy - Nelson-Field

From large-scale gaze tracking (ThinkTV Benchmark Series, Amplified Intelligence):

- **Attention is not viewability.** An MRC-viewable impression (50% of pixels, 1-2 seconds) frequently receives zero human gaze. Most digital ads get under ~2 seconds of active attention.
- **Active vs passive attention.** Active = eyes on the ad. Passive = eyes on screen, not the ad. Attention is a spectrum determining strength of memory encoding; passive exposure needs materially higher frequency to encode what one active exposure does.
- **Threshold and decay.** ~2.5 seconds of active attention was the original threshold for reliable memory encoding and short-term sales effect; more attention slows memory decay. 2025 revision (VCCP Media x Amplified): **~1.5 seconds can be enough if distinctive brand assets appear early**.
- **Attention elasticity.** Each platform has a UX-set ceiling on the attention it can yield (scroll speed, clutter, screen coverage). Creative cannot outperform the platform ceiling.
- **STAS (Short-Term Advertising Strength; 100 = no effect).** Active viewing: TV 144, Facebook 118, YouTube 116. On mobile: TV 161, Facebook 121, YouTube 137.
- **Screen coverage.** Ads at 100% of screen pixels generate ~2x the sales impact of ads at 50% coverage.
- Net implication: buy attentive reach, not impressions. Platform choice constrains outcomes before creative is considered.

## 2023-26 refinements to carry into work

- **Profit Ability 2 (Thinkbox/Ebiquity et al., 2024).** MMM meta-analysis of GBP 1.8bn UK spend: average short-term (13 weeks or less) profit ROI 1.87, rising to 4.11 including sustained effects to 24 months; ~60% of total profit effect lands after week 13. Re-grounds the long/short argument in econometrics rather than award entries.
- **Attention as currency.** Adelaide AU scores (case average +41% upper-funnel, +55% lower-funnel lift for AU-optimised buys), Nielsen x Adelaide unified reach + attention view (2025), dentsu trading effective attention in real time. IAB/MRC attention guidelines reportedly issued late 2025 (below 90% confidence on status).
- **Nelson-Field 2024-25.** The Attention Economy: A Category Blueprint (2024); "Hacking the Attention Economy" (2025): ~USD198bn/year wasted on low-attention media (VCCP Media x Amplified claim, 2025 - single-source figure); the 1.5-second-with-assets finding; passive attention rehabilitated as an efficiency flywheel where frequency is cheap.
- **The market inverted 60:40 in practice** (~69% activation by 2024 per WARC, below 90% confidence on exact split) with documented effectiveness decline. Assembled holds the line on context-adjusted 60:40.
- **Zero-click search.** published estimates put ~58-65% of Google queries ending without a click (contested, below 90% confidence on the exact figure); CTRs on AI Overview queries fell sharply. Brand (branded search, share of search) becomes the measurable residue of upper-funnel work; harvesting clicks is getting scarcer, which strengthens the brand case.
- **Retail media** absorbs activation budgets and often behaves like trade spend. Do not let RMN spend masquerade as brand investment in the 60:40 ledger.

## Known critiques (be ready for them)

- Sharp rejects the brand/activation dichotomy and the IPA awards evidence base (self-selection). Response: Profit Ability 2 reaches the same majority-long-term conclusion from econometrics.
- Sharp argues paying premiums for attention is wasteful since advertising works through low-involvement exposure. Response: attention floors verify reach is real; we do not buy dwell beyond the threshold.
- ESOV critics (IPA blog, 2020s) note SOV is hard to measure across fragmented media. Response: use share of search and Nielsen Ad Intel / Guideline SMI as proxies, state the measurement basis.
