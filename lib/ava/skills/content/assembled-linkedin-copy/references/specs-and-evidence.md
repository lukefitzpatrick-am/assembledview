# LinkedIn specs and evidence (verified July 2026)

## Specs
- Single image ad: intro text 600 max, truncates ~150 in feed. Headline 200 max, 70 recommended. Description 300 max, ~100 shown, mainly Audience Network.
- Video ad: same intro text; captions essential (sound-off default).
- Carousel: intro 255, card headline 45, 2-10 cards.
- Message ad (Sponsored Messaging): subject 60, body 1,500, CTA 20 chars. Unavailable to EU audiences; check current availability before recommending (below 90% confidence on current regional rules).
- Text ads: headline 25, description 75.
- Document ads: first pages preview; gate later pages for lead gen.
- Thought leader ads: boost a member's post; copy reads as the person, not the brand.

## Evidence and operating principles
- LinkedIn B2B Institute / Binet & Field: B2B effectiveness optimum ~46:54 brand-to-activation; the 95:5 rule - ~95% of the category is out-of-market at any time, so memory-building beats persuasion for most impressions.
- Buying groups, not individuals: B2B purchases involve multiple roles; vary the angle by role rather than writing one "decision maker" ad.
- Specific numbers and peer proof outperform superlatives on LinkedIn; first-person testimonial framing resonates (platform guidance and practitioner consensus).
- Frequency is naturally low and CPMs high: fewer, more distinct creatives beat many near-duplicates.
- Professional context does not mean rational-only: emotional and fame effects hold in B2B (marketing brain, commitment 8).
