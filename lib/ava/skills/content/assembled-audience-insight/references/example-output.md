# Example - audience insight from a Roy Morgan export

From the April 2026 "all people" export, brief: a caravan client wants to reach men 25-49 considering a first caravan. Numbers below are real values from the export; the client context is fictional.

---

AUDIENCE: Men 25-49, national. Roy Morgan Single Source Australia, April 2026 (n=11,820 unweighted men 25-49 of 59,706 total; 4.81m projected).

THE HEADLINE
The 25-49 male audience has quietly become an audio-first, YouTube-heavy group whose linear TV habit is 11 points thinner than the population's, but they have not gone anywhere hard to reach: radio still gets two in three of them every week.

WHAT STANDS OUT

1. Linear TV under-delivers this audience specifically.
   56% of men 25-49 watched linear FTA TV in the last 7 days vs 67% of all people 14+ (index 84). BVOD does not close the gap (index ~80 for 25-34s). Implication: a TV-led plan built on all-people CPMs quietly under-reaches the target; TV buys need BVOD and YouTube layered in, weighted to this cell, not bought on habit.

2. Podcasts are the sharpest over-index in the file, and it is a sub-group story.
   30% of men 25-34 and 30% of men 35-49 listened to podcasts in the last 7 days vs 19% of all people (index ~155). But men 14-24 sit at just 14%. Implication: podcasts are a 25-49 male signature behaviour, not a "young people" one. Host-read spots against drive-time and long-form shows put us in a high-attention, sound-on environment the marketing brain rates well above its CPM.

3. YouTube is the reach base, not the add-on.
   58% of men 25-34 and 65% of men 35-49 watched YouTube in the last 7 days (index 103-115 vs all people at 56%). Combined with radio (58-65% weekly), two channels alone approach the weekly reach a three-channel TV-led plan was buying. Implication: re-architect the plan around YouTube + radio as the reach spine, with linear TV as the attention/coverage layer in peak, not the foundation.

4. Do not confuse the index with the market.
   Music streaming sits at parity for 25-34s (index ~100) and reaches one in seven. Neither special nor scaled: a context play for creative at most, not a reach line.

REACH ARCHITECTURE
- YouTube (weekly reach ~60% of target): reach spine, 6-15s branded-early formats. 100% screen coverage placements preferred.
- Radio + podcasts (weekly audio ~74%): frequency and attention layer, drive time and host-reads.
- Linear TV + BVOD (weekly ~56%): peak coverage layer for launch bursts and co-viewing weight.
Estimated unduplicated weekly cover of the three: high 80s. Verify in the planning tool.

CREATIVE AND CEP DIRECTION
Weekend-trip and school-holiday entry points fit the audio moments (commute Friday, drive time). First-caravan consideration is a long cycle: brand early in every asset, consistent assets across YouTube and audio so the memory structure compounds.

WATCH-OUTS
- 14-24 male cells run n~4,900 nationally but thin out at state level - do not headline state x young male cells without checking n.
- This export is media consumption only; category attitudes and intention need a category export before we claim motivation insights.
- v% for YouTube and social is measured on people 18+, so 14-17s are excluded from those rows.
